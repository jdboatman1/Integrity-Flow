## The Voltage Problem (Resolved)

- **Issue:** Lost neutral on service entrance caused alternating leg voltage spikes up to 140V
  on one leg while the other dropped, then alternated
- **Effect:** Motors and devices (AC compressor, fridge, washer) ran inefficiently, drew excess
  current, cycled more frequently — inflated kWh consumption
- **Status:** One leg finally went out ~2 months ago, Oncor repaired it
- **Implication:** Historical bills do NOT reflect true baseline consumption. Post-repair bills
  will be the first accurate picture of actual usage

## Action Items Before Signing a Plan

1. Get 2–3 months of post-repair bills to confirm actual monthly kWh usage
2. Check usage history at **smartmetertexas.com** (Oncor smart meter data by day/hour)
3. Then choose a plan based on real consumption numbers

---

## Why This Matters for Plan Selection

Bill-credit plans (like Chariot GridPlus 15) only deliver their advertised rate if you
consistently hit the usage threshold (2,000 kWh). If post-repair consumption turns out to
be closer to 1,200–1,500 kWh, those plans backfire — you lose the credit AND pay a higher
base rate.

## True Cost Breakdown (what you actually pay)

The advertised rate is energy-only. Oncor delivery charges are added on top regardless
of which provider you pick:

| Component | Estimate at 2,000 kWh |
|---|---|
| Energy charge (e.g. Chariot 7.9¢ × 2,000) | ~$158 |
| Oncor per-kWh delivery (~4.5¢ × 2,000) | ~$90 |
| Oncor fixed monthly fee | ~$11 |
| **Estimated total** | **~$259/month** |

**Always check the Electricity Facts Label (EFL)** at powertochoose.org for the all-in
rate at 500, 1,000, and 2,000 kWh before signing. The EFL is the legal document.

---

## Free Nights/Weekends — Skip It

Not worth it for a typical Allen household. Daytime rates on those plans run 18–24¢/kWh.
At 2,000 kWh with Texas summer AC load, a fixed plan at 7.9¢ saves $25–$96/month over
any free nights/weekends option. Only worth considering for night-shift workers or EV
owners charging every night.

---

## Timing

Spring (March–April) is the best window to lock in a new plan — rates are at annual lows
before summer demand spikes. The window is still open. No need to rush before getting
actual post-repair bills.
