## Should You Get a Free Nights or Free Weekends Plan?

**Short answer: No — not for a typical household.**

### How These Plans Work

"Free" hours are usually 8 PM–6 AM (nights) or Friday evening through Monday morning
(weekends). During those windows, your energy charge is waived — but TDU delivery fees
(~5–6¢/kWh) still apply, so "free" electricity actually costs ~5–6¢/kWh. During all
other hours, you pay a premium rate.

### The Real Numbers

| Plan Type | Example Daytime Rate | Example Bill (1,000 kWh) | vs. Fixed Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixed-rate (Champion Saver) | 7.3¢/kWh all hours | ~$122/mo | baseline |
| Reliant Truly Free Nights | 18.2¢/kWh (6am–8pm) | ~$148/mo | +$26/mo |
| Reliant Truly Free Weekends | 17.8¢/kWh (weekdays) | ~$148/mo | +$26/mo |
| Direct Energy 12 Hour Power | 23.6¢/kWh (9am–9pm) | higher | +$50–$96/mo |

At 2,000 kWh the gap gets wider, not smaller — you're running more load during
peak daytime hours (AC in a Texas summer), so the high daytime rate hits you harder.

### Why the Math Rarely Works

Texas homes use roughly **65% of their electricity during daytime hours** — cooking,
laundry, HVAC when it's hot, working from home. Unless you can genuinely move more than
half your usage to the free window, the inflated daytime rate wipes out the savings and
then some.

### Who Free Nights/Weekends IS Worth It For

- Night-shift workers who are home and awake 9 PM–6 AM
- EV owners charging every night (2,000+ kWh/month from the car alone)
- Pool owners running pumps and heat only overnight
- Households that can realistically shift **>50% of usage** to free hours

### Who Should Stick With Fixed-Rate

- Families with kids at home during the day
- Work-from-home households
- Heavy AC users (Allen summers are brutal — your compressor runs all afternoon)
- Anyone whose schedule is a normal 8 AM–10 PM active window

### Bottom Line on Free Nights/Weekends

For the average Allen household at 2,000 kWh, a fixed-rate plan at 7.9–8.5¢/kWh
will almost certainly cost **$25–$96 less per month** than a free nights/weekends
plan. The marketing is compelling but the math is not. Stick with Chariot or APG&E.

## Recommendation

1. **Best overall:** Chariot Energy GridPlus 15 at 7.9¢/kWh — lowest rate, 100% solar,
   reasonable exit terms.
2. **Best if you want a simple flat contract:** APG&E SimpleSaver Smart Credit 16 at 8.0¢ —
   nearly as cheap, $125 monthly credit baked in.
3. **Best flexibility / lowest exit cost:** 4Change Energy Maxx Saver Value 12 — only $20
   to leave if something changes.

**Before signing any plan:** Enter your exact ZIP code on
[Power to Choose](https://www.powertochoose.org) (the official Texas PUC comparison site)
to pull the current Electricity Facts Label for your specific address. Rates shift weekly
and the EFL is the legal document — not the comparison sites.
